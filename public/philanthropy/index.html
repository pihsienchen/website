Philanthropy

Music education and humanitarian initiatives

Pi-hsien Chen firmly believes in music's transformative power and its ability to transcend cultural boundaries. Her philanthropic efforts focus on using music as a tool for healing, education, and social change, supporting both local and international organizations dedicated to improving human lives.

Supported Projects

MSF

Doctors Without Borders

Benefit concerts supporting medical aid in crisis zones. Music as a bridge of solidarity and healing.

NABU

NABU - Nature Conservation

Outdoor concerts raising environmental awareness. Connecting nature and music for sustainable future.

Bissau

Bissau Music Education Project

Music education for underprivileged children in Guinea-Bissau. Providing instruments, training, and hope through music.

Therapy

Music Therapy Programs

Therapeutic music sessions in hospitals and care facilities. Healing and comfort through music's power.

Youth

Youth Orchestra Support

Scholarships and mentoring for talented young musicians. Investing in the next generation of artists.

Hope

Refugee Music Program

Music workshops and concerts for refugee communities. Music as universal language of hope and integration.
